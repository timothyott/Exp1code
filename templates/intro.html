The following is a very simple business simulation where you will be playing the role of the entrepreneur leading a new company. Your task is to choose which opportunities for growth to pursue to successfully add more customers. In each round of the simulation you will be asked to choose one of three customer opportunities (or to choose to do nothing that round) and to choose how you plan on executing that opportunity. If you choose to do nothing your firm will only work on ongoing projects for that found. You will receive feedback between rounds on whether you have been successful or failed. There will be 20 decision rounds.

Product Description

The name of your company is Rental Property, Inc. The name of the product is Live With Me. You have created a new software platform meant to simplify living in and managing shared residences (apartments, houses, etc.). Your inspiration for the product came from personal experience dealing with roommates and landlords in shared living situations. The idea was spurred forward even more by recent reports that more people are renting longer and putting off buying places to live due to the economy and the continued trend towards holding multiple jobs in multiple areas of the country before settling down.

The roommate facing part of the product consists of a web/mobile interface. Each roommate creates an individual account for the service and is linked to the residence account created by one of the roommates and shared with the others. The residence link allows users to digitize various shared living activities. Currently the only feature built in is that roommates can enter expenses into the system to split the cost among them.

The landlord part of the product consists of a software solution that allows landlords to more efficiently manage their properties. Landlords can choose to interact with their residents via the ability to accept rent payments via credit card or PayPal, have renters submit online maintenance requests, and send out package notifications. You plan to continue to add to the functionality as customers request it.

Choices

Each round you will see descriptions of three opportunities for customers you could try and sell to. The customers vary in the type of residence, size, location, and the people that live there. The customers also vary in the features that they would want added before they will buy from you.

Along with choosing a customer you will have to make two choices about how to sell to that customer: Channel and Pricing. Channel refers to the way in which you will approach that customer. Pricing is how the customer will agree to pay you for the product. Different execution choices may be more effective for different opportunities.
